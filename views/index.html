SBL Rubric

Listening
I can listen to a student when they are in distress.
I can listen to a student when they are in distress and try to support them by helping them problem-solve that particular incident.
I can see patterns that lead to student distress and start to think of solutions to those issues.
I can bring up these patterns and issues to my team, department, and administration and start to organize changes that help to remedy systematic issues that cause student distress.
Difficult Conversations
I am open to a conversation about DEIB topics, if a student or adult brings them up.
I am open to a conversation about DEIB topics and ask questions about how to improve my work with my community.
I willingly take risks in my classroom to improve my work in DEIB, being open to mistakes.
I willingly take risks in my community to improve my work in DEIB and am willing to make public mistakes.
Opting In
I can opt-in to a situation when I see something with my own eyes.
I can opt-in to any situation, ask clarifying questions, and help a student feel heard in that moment.
I can opt-in to helping my community with DEIB issues if it feels like a good safe time to bring up an issue.
Ican opt-in every time for issues in DEIB, even when it feels hard, scary, or unsafe. After all, think about how my students feel.
Bias & Privilege
I can treat everyone in school as if they are equal with equal experiences.
I can begin to understand my own bias and privilege and what my particular lens brings to my teaching.
I can recognize and openly name my bias and privilege and adjust my teaching and practice accordingly.
I am willing to recognize privilege and power as it exists in myself and my community. I work to dismantle systemic racism.
